I am a PhD student in Transportation Engineering at the University of Mississippi (Ole Miss), working as a Graduate Research and Teaching Assistant under Dr. Amir M. Molan. My primary research focuses on NCHRP Project 17-131 — developing a national practitioner guide for Alternative Intersections and Interchanges (AII).

I hold a Master's in Transportation Engineering from Utah State University (GPA: 3.75) and bring experience across two countries — from UDOT-funded VRU safety research in Utah to roadway design projects in Nepal.

University of Mississippi · May 14, 2026
Engineering Professor Developing National Guide for Safer Roadways
The University of Mississippi highlights NCHRP Project 17-131, a $550,000 NCHRP-funded initiative led by Dr. Amir Molan to develop an updated national guide for Alternative Intersections and Interchanges (AII). The feature spotlights the research team — including Shailendra Khanal, whose doctoral dissertation focuses on the safety and operational performance of AII designs — and the project's goal of reducing the roughly 25% of fatal U.S. crashes that occur at or near intersections.
